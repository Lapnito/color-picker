## What is Color Picker: Camera HEX RGB?

**Color Picker: Camera HEX RGB** is a free color identification app by [lapnito.cz s.r.o.](https://lapnito.cz) that lets you capture exact colors from the real world using your phone's camera or any photo from your gallery. Point the crosshair at any surface, and the app instantly identifies the pixel color and provides its value in HEX, RGB, HSL, and CMYK formats.

Available as **Color Picker: Camera HEX RGB** on [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.tomas.color_picker) and as **Color Picker Camera: HEX RGB** on the [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/color-picker-camera-hex-rgb/id6759371237).

Whether you're a graphic designer matching a brand color from a physical sample, a developer extracting exact color codes for CSS, an interior decorator sampling wall paints, or a hobbyist building a mood board — Color Picker gives you precise, copy-ready color values in one tap. All processing happens locally on your device, with zero data collection and no ads.

## Key Features

## How Camera Color Picking Works

Color Picker uses your phone's rear camera to capture a live video feed. A precision crosshair overlay is positioned at the center of the screen, marking the exact pixel being sampled. As you move the camera, the app reads the color value of the pixel under the crosshair in real time and converts it into multiple color formats (HEX, RGB, HSL, CMYK) simultaneously.

For photo-based color picking, the app loads the selected image and allows you to tap on any pixel. The color at the tap coordinates is extracted from the image's pixel data and converted to all supported formats. This works with any image format supported by your device's photo library.

All color processing is performed locally on your device using the camera hardware and GPU. No images or color data are sent to any server. The app requires camera permission solely for live color picking and photo library access solely for gallery-based color extraction.

**How accurate is the camera color picker?** The accuracy depends on your camera sensor and lighting conditions. For best results, ensure good, neutral lighting and hold the device steady. The app captures the exact pixel color from the camera feed, so the accuracy is limited only by the camera hardware itself.

**Can I pick colors from any photo?** Yes, you can pick colors from any image stored on your device — photos, screenshots, downloaded images, and design mockups all work.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Color Picker free?**
A: Yes, the app is completely free to download and use on both Android and iOS. There are no ads and no in-app purchases.

**Q: Does it work without internet?**
A: Yes, Color Picker works completely offline. All color detection and processing happens locally on your device. No internet connection is needed.

**Q: What color formats does it support?**
A: The app supports HEX (e.g., #FF5733), RGB (e.g., 255, 87, 51), HSL (e.g., 11°, 100%, 60%), and CMYK (e.g., 0%, 66%, 80%, 0%). All formats are displayed simultaneously for every picked color.

**Q: Can I use it as an eyedropper tool?**
A: Yes, Color Picker works exactly like an eyedropper tool from desktop design software, but for the real world. Point your camera at any surface and the crosshair identifies the exact pixel color.

**Q: Does it collect my data?**
A: No. Color Picker does not collect any user data. All your colors, palettes, and settings are stored only on your device. There are no analytics, no tracking, and no ads.

**Q: Can I export my palettes for use in design tools?**
A: Yes, you can export palettes as JSON, CSS variables, text descriptions, or PNG images. This makes it easy to share colors with teammates or import them into Figma, Sketch, VS Code, or any other tool.

**Q: What is RAL matching?**
A: RAL is a standardized color system used primarily in painting, coatings, and industrial design. Color Picker finds the closest RAL color reference for any picked color, so you can identify the matching paint or coating.

**Q: Which devices are supported?**
A: Android 5.0 (Lollipop) and above, and iOS 13.0 and above. The app also runs on iPad, iPod touch, Mac with Apple Silicon, and Apple Vision.

**Q: What's the difference between the Android and iOS versions?**
A: Both versions offer the same core functionality. The Android version is called "Color Picker: Camera HEX RGB" and the iOS version is called "Color Picker Camera: HEX RGB."
